KareKare

Description

Kare-Kare is a delectable and iconic Filipino dish renowned for its rich and savory flavors. At its core, it's a sumptuous stew featuring tender oxtail, tripe, and a medley of vegetables like eggplant, string beans, and banana blossoms, all bathed in a luxuriously thick, peanut-based sauce. This creamy and nutty sauce is the star of the show, providing a delightful contrast to the natural sweetness of the vegetables. Kare-Kare is traditionally served with a side of shrimp paste (bagoong) and often enjoyed with fragrant rice, making it a beloved and indulgent feast that beautifully captures the essence of Filipino culinary diversity.

Ingredients

  • Oxtail
  • Eggplant
  • String Beans
  • banana blossom
  • Chinese eggplant
  • Ground roasted peanuts
  • Rice flour
  • Annatto oil
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Fish sauce
  • Salt and pepper
  • Water

Steps

  1. Boil the oxtail
  2. In a separate pot, blanch the eggplant, string beans, banana blossom, and Chinese eggplant until they are just slightly tender. Drain and set aside.
  3. Create the KareKare Sauce
    • In a separate pan, make the peanut sauce. Start by saut√©ing the garlic and onions in annatto oil until fragrant.
    • Add the ground roasted peanuts and rice flour, and stir continuously to make a roux. Gradually add water while stirring to create a smooth sauce.
    • Season the sauce with fish sauce, salt, and pepper according to your taste preferences. Continue to simmer until the sauce thickens.
  4. Add the boiled oxtail to the peanut sauce and simmer for a few more minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  5. Serve
