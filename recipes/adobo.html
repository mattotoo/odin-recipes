Adobo

Description

Chicken Adobo is a beloved Filipino culinary masterpiece that embodies the perfect harmony of flavors and simplicity. This iconic dish begins with succulent pieces of chicken, marinated in a unique blend of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and aromatic spices. The magic unfolds as the chicken simmers in this rich, dark, and tantalizingly fragrant sauce, resulting in a tender and savory delight that tantalizes the taste buds. The sweet and tangy notes of the vinegar are beautifully complemented by the umami depth of soy sauce, while garlic infuses its unmistakable warmth into every bite. The result is a culinary symphony that represents the heart and soul of Filipino cuisine, a mouthwatering masterpiece known for its unforgettable taste and universal appeal.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Soy sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Garlic
  • Black peppercorns
  • Bay leaves
  • Onion
  • Sugar

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken
    • In a large bowl or a zip-top bag, combine the chicken pieces, soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic, black peppercorns, bay leaves, sliced onion, and sugar (if using). Make sure the chicken is well coated with the marinade.
    • Seal the bag or cover the bowl and marinate the chicken in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour. For a more flavorful result, you can marinate it overnight.
  2. Cooking the chicken
    • Transfer the marinated chicken and the marinade into a large, heavy-bottomed pot or a deep skillet.
  3. Simmer
    • Place the pot over medium heat and bring the mixture to a boil.
    • Reduce the heat to low and let the chicken simmer, uncovered, for about 30-45 minutes. Stir occasionally to ensure the chicken is cooked evenly.
  4. Serve
